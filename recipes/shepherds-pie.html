Shepherd's Pie

Description

English readers should avert their eyes from the travesty that follows. This is not a recipe for a traditional shepherd's pie (it doesn't even have lamb in it!), but for us poor souls who don't know any better, it's a quick, easy, and delicious meal that's sure to please. Maybe another name would be better for this distinctly American evolution of the traditional recipe. Cowboy's Pie? Rancher's Pie? Whatever you call it, almost every family throughout the American South and Midwest has their own version of this classic. What follows is our family's way of doing it, passed down and altered by each generation going back as far as we can remember.

Ingredients

  • 2lbs ground beef (or lamb, or whatever suits your fancy)
  • 1 large can cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 bag shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 bag frozen corn (or peas, or vegetable mix)
  • Mashed potatoes
  • Parsley, sage, rosemary, thyme (yep)
  • Worcestershire sauce

Instructions

  1. Brown ground beef in a skillet, drain excess fat, and add seasoning.
  2. Mix together ground beef and cream of mushroom soup in a large baking dish, and spread it evenly along the bottom.
  3. Pour frozen corn on top and spread it evenly over the ground beef.
  4. Cover in a layer of shredded cheese.
  5. Spread mashed potatoes evenly on top of everything, smoothing it out to a small mound.
  6. Bake at 400 degrees Fahrenheit for 30 - 40 minutes, until mashed potatoes are starting to brown.
  7. Let cool at room temperature for 10 minutes, then serve!
